SECTOR 01: WAVE MECHANICS & FUNDAMENTALS
Define Wave Types (Mechanical vs EM)
Basic
Parameters: $A, f, \lambda, T$
Formula
Speed equation: $$v = f\lambda$$ Frequency: $$f = 1/T$$
Speed & Energy Relations
Conceptual
Energy is proportional to Amplitude squared: $$E \propto A^2$$ Speed depends on medium density/elasticity, NOT frequency.
SECTOR 02: SUPERPOSITION & INTERFERENCE
Principle of Superposition
Definition
Resultant displacement is vector sum: $$y = y_1 + y_2$$ Application: Noise-cancelling headphones.
Interference Conditions (Path Difference)
Critical
Constructive: $$\\Delta x = n\\lambda$$ Destructive: $$\\Delta x = (n + \\frac{1}{2})\\lambda$$
Beats Phenomenon
Numerical
Periodic loudness change. $$f_{beat} = |f_A - f_B|$$ Uses: Tuning forks, Radar speed guns.
SECTOR 03: STATIONARY WAVES (THE BIG GUNS)
Stretched String Analysis
Derivation
Speed on string: $$v = \sqrt{\\frac{F}{m}}$$ Harmonics: $$f_n = n f_1$$ Nodes at ends.
Air Column: Open Pipe
Derivation
Antinodes at both ends. All Harmonics present ($f, 2f, 3f...$). Length: $$L = \frac{n\\lambda}{2}$$
Air Column: Closed Pipe
Derivation
Node at closed end, Antinode at open. Only ODD Harmonics ($f, 3f, 5f...$). Length: $$L = \frac{n\\lambda}{4}$$ (for fundamental)
Microwave Experiment
Exp 7.7
Using microwave transmitter and detector to find Nodes/Antinodes. $$c = f\\lambda$$
SECTOR 04: ADVANCED PHENOMENA
Doppler Effect: Source Moving
Complex
Towards Observer ($\\lambda$ decreases): $$f' = f(\\"frac{v}{v-u_s})$$ Away from Observer ($\\lambda$ increases): $$f' = f(\\"frac{v}{v+u_s})$$
Doppler Applications
Theory
Radar, Sonar, Red Shift (Stars moving away), Blue Shift (Stars approaching).
Diffraction & Ripple Tank
Exp
