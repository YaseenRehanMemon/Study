Physics: Wave Motion

1. What is a Wave?

A wave is basically a traveling disturbance. It's the universe's way of moving energy without moving the "stuff" itself.

  • Transfers Energy from point $A$ to $B$.
  • No transfer of matter (the particles stay home).
  • Particles oscillate about a mean position.

2. The Foundation: Oscillatory Motion

Think of it this way: One particle dances (oscillation), and its neighbors decide to follow along. The "Wave" is just the collective result of many dancers!

3. Key Parameters

Amplitude ($A$)

Maximum displacement. More Amplitude = More Energy. Does NOT affect speed!

Frequency ($f$)

Oscillations per second. Measured in $\text{Hz}$.

$$f = \frac{1}{T}$$

Wavelength ($\lambda$)

Distance between two identical points (Crest to Crest).

Time Period ($T$)

Time for one full wiggle.

4. Wave Speed & Energy

The Golden Rule: Speed ($v$) depends ONLY on the medium (density & elasticity).

$$v = f \times \lambda$$

Energy Check: Energy is proportional to the Square of Amplitude.

$$E \propto A^2$$

5. Types of Waves

By Nature

  • Mechanical: Needs stuff (Sound, Water).
  • EM Waves: Vacuum okay! (Light, X-rays).
  • Quantum: Probability waves.

By Motion

  • Transverse: Perpendicular wiggle (String).
  • Longitudinal: Parallel wiggle (Sound).

6. Principle of Superposition

When two or more waves overlap, the resulting displacement is the vector sum of individual displacements.

$$y = y_1 + y_2 + y_3 + \dots$$

This principle gives rise to three major phenomena depending on the wave properties:

  • Interference: Same frequency, constant phase difference.
  • Beats: Slightly different frequencies (heard as volume pulsing).
  • Stationary Waves: Same frequency, traveling in opposite directions.

7. Interference in Detail

Redistribution of energy due to superposition of coherent waves (same $f$, fixed phase).

Constructive

Waves meet In-Phase (Crest meets Crest).

  • Max Amplitude: $A_{\text{max}} = A_1 + A_2$
  • Max Intensity: $I \propto (A_1 + A_2)^2$
  • Path Diff: $\Delta x = n\lambda$
  • Phase Diff: $\phi = 2n\pi$

Destructive

Waves meet in Opposite Phase (Crest meets Trough).

  • Min Amplitude: $A_{\text{min}} = |A_1 - A_2|$
  • Min Intensity: $I \propto (A_1 - A_2)^2$
  • Path Diff: $\Delta x = (2n+1)\frac{\lambda}{2}$
  • Phase Diff: $\phi = (2n+1)\pi$

8. Superposition Phenomena

A. Beats

When two sound waves of slightly different frequencies ($f_1 \approx f_2$) overlap.

  • Result: Periodic rise and fall in loudness.
  • Beat Frequency: $f_b = |f_1 - f_2|$

B. Stationary (Standing) Waves

Formed by superposition of two identical waves traveling in opposite directions in a confined medium.

  • Nodes (N): Points of zero amplitude (total destructive).
  • Antinodes (A): Points of max amplitude (total constructive).
  • Energy is trapped—it does not propagate!
Cool Tip: Path difference ($\Delta x$) is the "head start" one wave has over another. If that head start is exactly one full wavelength ($\lambda$), they stay perfectly aligned (Constructive)!
