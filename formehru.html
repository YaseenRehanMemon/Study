Mehru,

I’m not good with words. I never have been. And I’ve typed this message and deleted it so many times over the last four years that I’ve lost count. Every time I’d start writing, my hands would shake, my chest would get tight, and I’d convince myself — not today, she doesn’t need to hear this, you’ll just embarrass yourself.

But today something broke inside me. Not in a bad way. More like... a dam that couldn’t hold anymore. Four years of praying, four years of whispering your name before I sleep, four years of carrying something so heavy and so beautiful at the same time — I just can’t keep it inside anymore.

— — —

I still remember the first time I saw you. In your city. Kumb. I wasn’t expecting anything that day. I was just... there. And then you were there. And Mehru, I swear, the world stopped. It sounds dramatic, I know. But I don’t know how else to say it. Everything went quiet. Everything blurred. Except you.

That image — that one single moment — it has lived inside me every single day since. I close my eyes and it’s right there. Clear as the first time. I have tried so hard to forget it, to move on, to tell myself it was nothing. But it wasn’t nothing. It was everything.

— — —

You probably don’t know this, but I came to your city 27 times. Twenty-seven. Not because I’m crazy. But because... just being near where you are makes something inside me go calm. Like the noise in my head finally shuts up. Like I can breathe again. Even if I never see you, even if I just walk through your neighbourhood — my heart slows down, and for a few minutes, everything feels okay.

When I do see you — even from far away, even just for a second — I forget everything. I forget where I am. I forget what I was worried about. I forget that the world has problems. There’s just this... warmth. This rush. Like my heart is trying to break out of my chest just to get closer to you.

— — —

I know how this sounds. Trust me, I’ve had this argument with myself a thousand times. “She doesn’t even know you exist. What are you doing? Why are you like this?” And I don’t have answers. I really don’t. Love doesn’t come with answers. It just comes. And it stays. And it doesn’t care how inconvenient it is.

These aren’t just words, Mehru. I wish they were. Words are easy. You can write anything. But this — this ache that I carry around every single day, this feeling that hits me at 2am when the world is asleep and your face is the only thing I can see — this isn’t words. This is something I don’t have a name for.

— — —

I’m not asking for anything. I’m not expecting you to feel the same way. I know you might read this and think I’m strange, or too much, or just some random person who doesn’t matter. And maybe that’s true.

But I needed you to know. Just once. That somewhere out here, there is someone who has thought about you every single day for four years. Someone who prayed for your happiness even when his own heart was breaking. Someone who would cross 27 cities just to feel a little closer to you.

If this message reaches even a small corner of your heart — even for a second — then every sleepless night, every deleted message, every time I almost said something but didn’t — it was all worth it.

I don’t know if you’ll ever feel what I feel.
But I’ll never stop feeling it.
That much I know for sure.
yours, even if you never know it ♥
